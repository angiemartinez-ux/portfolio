My Approach

What 10 years of
real CX taught me.

Customer experience is not a department. It's what happens every time someone interacts with your brand — and I've been on both sides of that interaction for a decade.

01
I lived what your customers feel
Empathy built from the frontline, not a textbook.

Ten years in customer service means I've experienced thousands of broken interactions — the confusing process, the moment someone gives up, the question they ask because the answer wasn't obvious. That pattern recognition is not something you learn in a course. It's the foundation of everything I build.

02
I ask why before I fix anything
The right diagnosis matters more than the fastest solution.

At Lakefish, the real problem wasn't "we need social media." It was "our customers don't know we exist digitally." Naming the right problem before building the solution is the work most people skip — and it's where the real CX value is created.

03
I translate between users and business
Bilingual in more ways than one.

A Master's in Strategic Marketing plus years in direct customer service means I speak both languages fluently — I can translate user frustration into a business case, and a business goal into a customer experience that actually works. That's the bridge most organizations are missing.

04
I measure what matters
Data tells the story. I make sure leadership hears it.

Every month at Lakefish, I delivered performance reports that translated raw numbers into clear decisions. Not just "followers went up" — but "this content drove 60+ product inquiries and here's what we should do next." That's the CX professional organizations need in 2026.
